# Emmanuel Levinas (1906–1995)

## Life

Levinas was born in Kaunas, Lithuania, into a Jewish family. He studied phenomenology under Husserl and Heidegger. During World War II, he was a prisoner of war; his family was murdered in the Holocaust.

After the war, he directed a Jewish teacher training school and eventually became professor in Paris. He wrote philosophical works and Talmudic commentaries until his death in 1995.

## Major Works

- **Totality and Infinity** (1961)
- **Otherwise than Being** (1974)

---

## Core Philosophy

### Ethics as First Philosophy
Western philosophy has been ontology. Levinas reverses this: **ethics is first philosophy**. Before Being, there is the relation to the Other.

### The Face (Visage)
The face of the Other is the site of the ethical. The face speaks: **"Thou shalt not kill."** It is naked, vulnerable, exposed. It commands and makes me responsible.

### Responsibility
I am responsible for the Other—infinitely, asymmetrically, before any contract. This responsibility constitutes subjectivity itself.

### Against Totality
The Other exceeds every system. The face breaks totality, opens infinity.
