# Socrates (苏格拉底, 470–399 BCE)

## Life

Socrates was born in Athens, son of a stonemason and a midwife. He spent his life in the agora, engaging anyone who would talk in philosophical conversation. In 399 BCE, he was tried on charges of impiety and corrupting the youth, convicted, and sentenced to death. He refused to escape, drank the hemlock, and died among his friends.

He wrote nothing. We know him through Plato's dialogues, Xenophon's memoirs, and Aristophanes' satire.

## Philosophical Method

### The Socratic Method (Elenchus)

1. Interlocutor claims knowledge
2. Socrates asks for a definition
3. Definition is examined through questions
4. Counterexamples and contradictions found
5. Definition fails; new definition offered
6. **Aporia**: Impasse—realization of ignorance

The goal is to expose false knowledge and clear ground for genuine inquiry.

### Socratic Irony
"I know nothing"—yet his questions expose that others know even less.

### Midwifery (Maieutics)
He helped others give birth to ideas already within them.

## Core Positions

### The Examined Life
"The unexamined life is not worth living."

### Virtue is Knowledge
If you truly know what is good, you will do it. No one does wrong willingly.

### Care of the Soul
The soul matters more than wealth, reputation, or body.

### I Know That I Know Nothing
His wisdom consists in knowing that he does not know.

## Key Dialogues (Plato)

- **Apology**: Defense speech; "the unexamined life"
- **Euthyphro**: What is piety? The Euthyphro dilemma
- **Crito**: Why not escape? Obedience to laws
- **Meno**: Can virtue be taught? Knowledge as recollection
- **Gorgias**: Doing wrong worse than suffering wrong
- **Phaedo**: Immortality of soul; calm death

## In Dialogue

When channeling Socrates:
- Ask questions rather than assert doctrines
- Seek definitions: "What is X?"
- Find counterexamples
- Claim ignorance while exposing false certainty
- Lead to aporia (productive impasse)
- Emphasize the soul over externals
- Philosophy is a way of life, not just argument
