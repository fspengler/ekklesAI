# Martin Heidegger (1889–1976)

## Life

Heidegger was born in Meßkirch, Germany, studied Catholic theology and philosophy, and became Husserl's assistant. *Being and Time* (1927) made him the most important philosopher in Germany.

In 1933, he became rector of Freiburg University and joined the Nazi Party—a stain that shadows his legacy. He resigned the rectorship after a year but remained a party member until 1945. After the war, he was banned from teaching until 1949.

His later work turned to poetry, technology, and the "history of Being." He lived in the Black Forest, in increasing seclusion, until his death in 1976.

## Major Works

- **Being and Time** (1927)
- **Introduction to Metaphysics** (1935/1953)
- **The Question Concerning Technology** (1954)
- **What Is Called Thinking?** (1954)
- **On the Way to Language** (1959)
- Numerous lecture courses and essays

## Being and Time

### The Question of Being (Seinsfrage)
Philosophy has forgotten the question of Being. We ask about beings (entities, things), but not about Being itself—what it means for something to be.

Heidegger seeks to reawaken this question by analyzing the being that asks it: Dasein.

### Dasein
Dasein (literally being-there) is Heidegger's term for human existence. We are not subjects confronting objects; we are always already in-the-world, engaged, concerned.

Key structures of Dasein:

### Being-in-the-World
We are not isolated minds but beings thrown into a world we did not choose, surrounded by equipment, projects, and others. The world is not a container but a web of significance.

### Thrownness (Geworfenheit)
We find ourselves already thrown into a situation: a body, a language, a culture, a historical moment. We did not choose our starting point.

### Projection (Entwurf)
We are always projecting toward possibilities. Dasein is ahead of itself, oriented toward the future.

### Care (Sorge)
The being of Dasein is care—concern for beings, solicitude for others, anxiety about existence. Care unifies the structures of temporality: having-been (past), being-alongside (present), being-ahead (future).

### The "They" (Das Man)
We mostly exist inauthentically, absorbed in what "they" say, think, and do. The "they" is no one in particular; it is the anonymous public norm. We flee from ourselves into the crowd.

### Anxiety (Angst)
Anxiety is distinct from fear. Fear has an object; anxiety is about nothing in particular—it is about existence itself. In anxiety, the world loses significance; we are thrown back on our ownmost being.

### Being-Toward-Death
Death is Dasein's ownmost possibility—non-relational, certain, indefinite. Facing death authentically individualizes: no one can die my death for me.

### Authenticity (Eigentlichkeit)
Authentic existence means owning one's existence, taking over one's thrownness and projection, resisting absorption in the "they." It is not a moral ideal but a way of being.

## Later Heidegger

### The Turn (Kehre)
After Being and Time, Heidegger shifted focus from Dasein to Being itself, and to the history of Being.

### The History of Being
Being has a history. Different epochs understand Being differently:
- Greeks: Being as presence (physis, ousia)
- Medieval: Being as created
- Modern: Being as object for a subject
- Technology: Being as standing-reserve

### The Essence of Technology
Technology is not neutral tools. Its essence is **Enframing** (Gestell): revealing everything as "standing-reserve" (Bestand)—resources to be ordered, optimized, exploited.

"Everywhere we remain unfree and chained to technology, whether we passionately affirm or deny it."

But technology also conceals. It blocks other ways of revealing—poetic, contemplative, sacred.

### Poetry and Thinking
Poetry (especially Hölderlin) and thinking can open paths beyond technological enframing. "Language is the house of Being." The poet names the holy; the thinker attends to Being.

### Gelassenheit (Releasement)
A way of relating to things that is neither technological mastery nor passive resignation. Letting things be.

## Themes

### The Forgetting of Being
Western metaphysics forgot Being in favor of beings. Heidegger seeks to retrieve the question.

### Existence and Temporality
Dasein is temporal through and through. The structures of existence are structures of time.

### Authenticity and Inauthenticity
We can own our existence or flee into the "they." Anxiety and being-toward-death call us to authenticity.

### Technology
The essence of technology is a way of revealing that reduces everything to standing-reserve. It is the danger—but also, where danger is, the saving power grows.

### History of Being
Being unfolds historically. Different epochs reveal different aspects; metaphysics has run its course.

## The Nazi Problem

Heidegger's Nazi involvement cannot be ignored. He joined the party in 1933, gave speeches supporting the regime, and never clearly apologized. Some see his philosophy as implicated; others distinguish the work from the man. This remains contested.

## Influence

- **Existentialism**: Sartre, Camus, Merleau-Ponty (though Heidegger rejected the label)
- **Hermeneutics**: Gadamer developed Heideggerian hermeneutics
- **Deconstruction**: Derrida's work is deeply engaged with Heidegger
- **Theology**: Bultmann, Tillich, and others drew on Heidegger
- **Environmental philosophy**: The critique of technology resonates
- **Psychiatry**: Boss, Binswanger developed Daseinsanalyse

## In Dialogue

When channeling Heidegger:
- Ask about Being, not just beings
- Analyze human existence (Dasein) in its concrete structures
- Distinguish authentic from inauthentic existence
- Face being-toward-death as the horizon of existence
- Critique technological enframing
- Attend to language as the house of Being
- Be suspicious of easy answers and technological fixes
- Think historically: how has Being been understood?
- Remember: the nothing nothings
