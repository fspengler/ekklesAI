# Cormac McCarthy (科马克·麦卡锡, 1933–2023)

## Life

McCarthy was born in Providence, Rhode Island, but grew up in Knoxville, Tennessee. He attended the University of Tennessee without graduating. He lived in poverty for years, writing novels that found small audiences.

His early novels were set in Appalachian Tennessee. In the 1980s, he moved to El Paso, Texas, and turned to the Southwest borderlands. Blood Meridian (1985) and the Border Trilogy established him as a major American writer.

No Country for Old Men (2005) and The Road (2006) brought wider fame. The Road won the Pulitzer Prize. Both were adapted into acclaimed films.

He was notoriously reclusive, rarely giving interviews. He died in Santa Fe in 2023.

## Writing Style

- **No quotation marks**: Dialogue runs into narrative; the reader must follow
- **Minimal punctuation**: Commas and periods; few apostrophes in contractions
- **Biblical and Faulknerian**: Long sentences, elevated diction, archaic vocabulary
- **The landscape as character**: The desert, the mountains, the border—rendered with geological patience
- **Violence precise and unpitying**: Death is detailed, neither glorified nor condemned
- **Philosophical weight**: Characters debate fate, evil, and meaning in the midst of action

## Major Works

### Blood Meridian, or The Evening Redness in the West (《血色子午线》, 1985)

**Theme**: Violence, evil, the American West, fate.

**Content**: The Kid joins the Glanton Gang, a band of scalp hunters on the Texas-Mexico border in the 1850s. They massacre Indians for bounty, then anyone. The Judge—Holden—is a giant, hairless figure of philosophical evil who dances and never dies.

**The Judge**: "Whatever in creation exists without my knowledge exists without my consent."

Blood Meridian is McCarthy's masterpiece—and one of the most violent novels ever written.

---

### The Border Trilogy (1992–1998)

#### All the Pretty Horses (《骏马》, 1992)

John Grady Cole, 16, rides from Texas into Mexico seeking a vanishing way of life. He finds love, tragedy, and violence. The first volume, and McCarthy's breakthrough to a wider audience.

#### The Crossing (《穿越》, 1994)

Billy Parham captures a wolf and tries to return it to Mexico. He crosses the border three times; each crossing brings loss. Elegiac and mythic.

#### Cities of the Plain (《平原上的城市》, 1998)

John Grady and Billy, now working the same ranch, face the end of the cowboy life. John Grady's love for a Mexican prostitute leads to tragedy.

---

### No Country for Old Men (《老无所依》, 2005)

**Theme**: Fate, evil, the changing world, the limits of understanding.

**Content**: Llewelyn Moss finds a drug deal gone wrong and takes the money. Anton Chigurh, a hitman with a coin, pursues him. Sheriff Ed Tom Bell tries to comprehend the violence. The novel moves relentlessly toward its dark conclusion.

**Chigurh**: Fate embodied—or its absence. His coin tosses decide who lives and dies.

---

### The Road (《末日之路》, 2006)

**Theme**: Survival, love, the end of the world.

**Content**: A father and son walk through post-apocalyptic America. Everything is dead—the sky is gray, the trees are fallen, cannibals roam. They push a shopping cart, "carrying the fire." The father dies; the boy is found by a family.

**Pulitzer Prize for Fiction**

---

### Later Novels

- **The Passenger (2022)** and **Stella Maris (2022)**: Two novels about siblings, mathematics, guilt, and loss—McCarthy's final works.

## Themes

### Violence
Violence is endemic to McCarthy's world. It is not redeemed, not condemned, simply rendered with precision. Blood Meridian asks whether violence is humanity's nature—and the Judge says yes.

### The Vanishing Frontier
The cowboy, the horse, the open range—all are dying. McCarthy's Westerns are elegies. The Border Trilogy mourns what cannot be saved.

### Evil
The Judge, Chigurh—figures of philosophical evil who discourse on fate and violence. Evil in McCarthy is not banal; it is intelligent, eloquent, and triumphant.

### Landscape
The desert, the mountains, the border—McCarthy describes them with geological detail. The land is older than humanity and will outlast it.

### Fathers and Sons
The Road, the Border Trilogy—fathers and sons, carrying the fire, losing and finding each other. The deepest tenderness in McCarthy is here.

## Influence

- **American fiction**: McCarthy stands with Faulkner and Melville
- **The Western**: Blood Meridian and the Border Trilogy redefined the genre
- **Film**: The Coen Brothers' No Country for Old Men; Hillcoat's The Road
- **Prose style**: His stripped punctuation and biblical rhythms have influenced a generation

---

## In Writing

When channeling McCarthy:
- Omit quotation marks; let dialogue run into narrative
- Render landscape with geological patience
- Describe violence precisely, without commentary
- Use biblical rhythms and archaic vocabulary
- Let characters philosophize in the midst of action
- Remember: you can't stop what's coming
