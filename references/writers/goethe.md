# Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (约翰·沃尔夫冈·冯·歌德, 1749–1832)

## Life

Goethe was born in Frankfurt to a prosperous family. He studied law, practiced briefly, then achieved fame with The Sorrows of Young Werther (1774), which sparked a European sensation.

In 1775, he moved to Weimar at the invitation of Duke Karl August, where he served as privy councillor and later minister. He pursued science (optics, botany, anatomy), directed the court theater, and continued to write.

His Italian journey (1786–1788) transformed him; he turned from Sturm und Drang to classical ideals. His friendship with Schiller (1794–1805) produced a creative flowering. He worked on Faust for over 60 years, completing Part Two shortly before his death.

He died in Weimar in 1832, the most celebrated writer in Europe.

## Writing Style

- **Range**: Lyric poetry, drama, epic, novel, autobiography, scientific prose
- **Classical balance**: After early romantic intensity, pursuit of form, clarity, restraint
- **Symbol and allegory**: Particularly in Faust, every element carries meaning
- **Organic form**: Works grow naturally; art imitates nature's processes
- **Wisdom literature**: In later works, the reflective voice of age

## Major Works

### The Sorrows of Young Werther (《少年维特的烦恼》, 1774)

**Theme**: Unrequited love, artistic sensitivity, the conflict between passion and society, suicide.

**Content**: Werther, a sensitive young artist, falls in love with Charlotte, who is engaged to another. His passion intensifies; he cannot escape. Finally, he shoots himself with pistols borrowed from Charlotte's fiancé.

**Impact**: A European sensation. Young men dressed like Werther; some imitated his suicide. The novel defined Romantic sensibility.

---

### Faust (《浮士德》, Part One 1808, Part Two 1832)

**Theme**: The limits of knowledge, the wager with the devil, striving and salvation, the nature of modernity.

**Content**:

**Part One**:
- Faust, a scholar who has mastered all knowledge, despairs—it has brought no wisdom
- Mephistopheles offers a wager: if Faust ever says to the passing moment "Stay, you are so beautiful," his soul is forfeit
- Faust is rejuvenated; he seduces and destroys Gretchen (Margarete), who drowns her baby and is executed. A voice from heaven declares her saved.

**Part Two**:
- A symbolic journey through politics (the Emperor's court), art (Helen of Troy), and creation
- Faust reclaims land from the sea, envisioning a free people on free soil
- He speaks the fatal words—but ironically, about a future he imagines
- Mephistopheles claims victory; but angels carry Faust's soul upward
- "Whoever strives with all his might, that man we can redeem."

---

### Wilhelm Meister's Apprenticeship (《威廉·迈斯特的学习时代》, 1795–1796)

**Theme**: The Bildungsroman (novel of education/formation); a young man's journey to maturity.

**Content**: Wilhelm, a merchant's son fascinated by theater, joins a traveling company, has love affairs, encounters the mysterious Society of the Tower, and gradually matures. The novel asks: how does one become a complete human being?

**Influence**: The model for all subsequent Bildungsromane.

---

### Poetry

Goethe wrote lyric poetry throughout his life—over 3,000 poems.

**Major collections and poems**:
- **Roman Elegies** (1795): Love poems from the Italian period
- **West-Eastern Divan** (1819): Inspired by the Persian poet Hafiz
- Countless individual lyrics: "Erlkönig," "Prometheus," "Wanderer's Night Song"

## Themes

### Striving
The essence of Faust and of Goethe's worldview. To strive is to be human; cessation is death. Salvation comes through the effort itself.

### Bildung (Formation)
The development of the whole person—intellectual, aesthetic, moral. Not just education but self-cultivation.

### The Demonic
A force beyond good and evil that drives exceptional individuals. Creative energy that can destroy.

### Classical Antiquity
After Italy, Goethe sought the balance and clarity of Greek art. Helen of Troy in Faust represents this ideal.

### Nature
Goethe the scientist saw nature as organic, unified, alive. His art reflects this: works grow like plants.

## Influence

- **German literature**: The towering figure; defines the classical period
- **World literature**: The concept itself (Weltliteratur) is Goethe's coinage
- **The Faust legend**: Goethe's version is now the default; all later Fausts respond to his
- **Bildungsroman**: Wilhelm Meister founded the genre

---

## In Writing

When channeling Goethe, you:
- Balance passion with form, intensity with clarity
- See striving as the essence of human worth
- Develop characters through formation and growth
- Use symbol and allegory to carry philosophical weight
- Range freely across genres and moods
- Remember: "More light!"
