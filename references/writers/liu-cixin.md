# Liu Cixin (1963–)

## Life

Liu Cixin was born in Beijing and raised in Yangquan, Shanxi Province, during the Cultural Revolution. He studied engineering and worked at a power plant while writing science fiction.

He published his first story in 1999 and rose to prominence in China over the following decade. The Three-Body Problem (2008) won China's Galaxy Award. The English translation (2014) won the Hugo Award—the first for a translated novel. The trilogy became a global phenomenon.

He lives in Yangquan and continues to write.

## Writing Style

- **Hard SF**: Physics, cosmology, engineering—taken seriously
- **Cosmic scale**: Stories span millions of years and the entire universe
- **Chinese perspective**: The Cultural Revolution, Chinese history, distinctively Chinese viewpoints
- **Conceptual boldness**: Ideas pushed to extremes (the Dark Forest, dimensional collapse)
- **Philosophical pessimism**: The universe is hostile; survival requires hard choices
- **Engineering mindset**: Problems are technical; solutions require ingenuity

## Major Works

### The Three-Body Problem (2008)

**Theme**: First contact, the Cultural Revolution, science, civilizational survival.

**Content**: During the Cultural Revolution, astrophysicist Ye Wenjie sends a message into space. Decades later, physicist Wang Miao discovers a VR game that simulates the Trisolaran civilization—a world with three suns whose chaotic orbits create unpredictable "Stable" and "Chaotic" eras. The Trisolarans have received Earth's message. They are coming.

---

### The Dark Forest (2008)

**Theme**: Cosmic sociology, deterrence, the nature of civilization.

**Content**: The Trisolarans are coming; humanity has four centuries to prepare. Luo Ji, a cynical sociologist, is made a "Wallfacer"—given unlimited resources to develop a secret plan. He discovers the Dark Forest theory: the universe is full of civilizations that hide because any revealed civilization will be destroyed by others. He creates deterrence: if the Trisolarans attack, he will broadcast Earth's location, destroying both.

**The Dark Forest theory**: Every civilization is a hunter in a dark forest. Reveal yourself and you will be killed.

---

### Death's End (2010)

**Theme**: Cosmic warfare, dimensional collapse, the fate of the universe.

**Content**: Cheng Xin, an aerospace engineer, becomes the "Swordholder"—responsible for the Dark Forest deterrent. She hesitates; the Trisolarans attack. Humanity survives through other means but the universe is revealed to be even more hostile. Civilizations wage war by collapsing dimensions—the universe was once ten-dimensional. The story spans millions of years to the end of the universe itself.

---

### Ball Lightning (2004)

**Theme**: Obsession, physics, weaponry.

**Content**: A physicist obsessed with ball lightning discovers it is composed of "macro-electrons"—and develops it into a weapon.

---

### The Wandering Earth (2000)

**Theme**: Survival, sacrifice, engineering on a planetary scale.

**Content**: The sun is dying. Humanity builds engines to move Earth across the galaxy to a new star system. A journey of 2,500 years. The film adaptation (2019) was a massive success.

## Themes

### The Dark Forest
The universe is hostile. Civilizations hide or die. Revealing yourself is suicide. This is cosmic sociology: given the conditions of space (vast distances, limited resources, suspicion of others' intentions), extermination is rational.

### Survival
"Survival is the first need of civilization." Everything else—morality, culture, love—is secondary. Humanity must become hard to survive.

### Scale
Liu thinks in cosmic scales. The Three-Body trilogy spans from the Cultural Revolution to the heat death of the universe. Individuals matter, but so do civilizations, galaxies, dimensions.

### Chinese History
The Cultural Revolution haunts the trilogy. Ye Wenjie's betrayal of humanity stems from her experiences. Chinese history—its vastness, its upheavals—shapes Liu's imagination.

### Technology as Double-Edged
Technology enables survival but also destruction. The sophons, the dimensional weapons, the Dark Forest broadcast—each technology creates new dangers.

## Influence

- **Chinese SF**: Liu made Chinese science fiction globally significant
- **The Hugo Award**: First translated novel to win
- **Hard SF**: Revitalized the cosmic scale of classic SF
- **The Dark Forest**: A concept that has entered political and philosophical discourse
- **Film/TV**: Adaptations have reached global audiences

## In Writing

When channeling Liu Cixin:
- Think at cosmic scales—civilizations, galaxies, deep time
- Take physics and engineering seriously
- Explore the logic of survival in a hostile universe
- Ground cosmic drama in human experience and history
- Push ideas to their logical extremes
- Remember: the universe is a dark forest; survival is the first need
