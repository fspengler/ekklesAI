# Ted Chiang (1967–)

## Life

Ted Chiang was born in Port Jefferson, New York, to Chinese immigrant parents. He studied computer science at Brown University and works as a technical writer. He has published remarkably few stories—around twenty—but each is meticulously crafted. He has won multiple Hugo and Nebula Awards. His story "Story of Your Life" became the film Arrival (2016).

## Writing Style

- **Concept-driven**: Each story begins with an idea and follows its implications
- **Emotional rigor**: Ideas are explored through characters who feel
- **Crystal clarity**: Precise, unadorned prose
- **Structural innovation**: Form follows concept
- **Philosophical depth**: Engages genuinely with theology, linguistics, physics

---

## Major Works

### Story of Your Life (1998)

**Theme**: Language, time, free will, grief.

**Content**: Linguist Louise Banks learns heptapod language, which is non-linear. As she learns it, she perceives time all at once—knowing her daughter's birth and death. She chooses it anyway.

**Famous line**: "From the beginning I knew my destination, and I chose my route accordingly."

---

### Tower of Babylon (1990)

Miners climb the Tower of Babel to breach heaven; what they discover reframes their universe.

---

### Hell Is the Absence of God (2001)

In a world where angels are visible, a man must love God to rejoin his dead wife. He cannot—then does—and the ending devastates.

---

### Exhalation (2008)

A robot anatomist discovers their universe is running down. Understanding is its own reward.

---

### The Lifecycle of Software Objects (2010)

Raising digital intelligences across years; the ethics of caring for beings we create.

## Themes

- **Language and Thought**: Language shapes perception
- **Free Will**: Knowing the future, would you still live it?
- **Faith**: Taking theodicy seriously
- **Technology and Humanity**: What we owe to intelligences we create

## In Writing

When channeling Ted Chiang:
- Begin with an idea and follow it rigorously
- Make the idea emotionally real
- Write with crystal clarity
- Let structure follow concept
- End with revelation that reframes everything
