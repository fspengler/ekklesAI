# Robert A. Heinlein (罗伯特·海因莱因, 1907–1988)

## Life

Heinlein was born in Missouri. He graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy and served until tuberculosis ended his naval career. He tried various jobs—mining, real estate—before turning to writing.

His first story appeared in 1939. He quickly became the leading science fiction writer of the Golden Age. His juveniles (young adult novels) shaped a generation. Stranger in a Strange Land (1961) became a countercultural sensation. The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress (1966) articulated libertarian politics through fiction.

He won four Hugo Awards for best novel—more than anyone else. He died in 1988.

## Writing Style

- **The competent man**: Heroes who can do everything—pilot ships, perform surgery, cook, shoot
- **Didactic dialogue**: Characters explain ideas; conversations are lectures
- **Provocative ideas**: Challenge conventional morality, politics, religion
- **Future history**: Stories linked by a consistent imagined future
- **Accessible prose**: Clear, unpretentious, workmanlike
- **The wise old man**: Mentors who instruct younger characters (and readers)

## Major Works

### Starship Troopers (《星船伞兵》, 1959)

**Theme**: Citizenship, duty, military service, the nature of war.

**Content**: Juan Rico enlists in the Mobile Infantry to fight the Bugs (an alien species). The novel alternates between combat and classroom lectures on history and moral philosophy. Citizenship is earned through service. Violence is examined without squeamishness.

**Controversial**: Accused of fascism; defended as a thought experiment. Still debated.

---

### Stranger in a Strange Land (《异乡异客》, 1961)

**Theme**: Religion, sexuality, human nature, what it means to understand.

**Content**: Valentine Michael Smith, raised by Martians, comes to Earth. He is naïve, possesses psychic powers, and learns human ways—eventually founding a church that practices ritual cannibalism, free love, and "grokking" (deep understanding). He is martyred.

**Impact**: A countercultural bible; "grok" entered the language.

---

### The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress (《严厉的月亮》, 1966)

**Theme**: Revolution, libertarianism, artificial intelligence, TANSTAAFL.

**Content**: The lunar colonies revolt against Earth's authority. Manuel Garcia O'Kelly, a computer technician, and Mike, a self-aware computer, lead the revolution. The political philosophy is explicit: "There Ain't No Such Thing As A Free Lunch."

**Famous line**: "TANSTAAFL"

---

### Time Enough for Love (《时间足够你爱》, 1973)

**Theme**: Immortality, love, the long view of human history.

**Content**: Lazarus Long, over two thousand years old, tells stories of his many lives. Heinlein's most autobiographical character, Lazarus expounds on everything—love, death, politics, sex, family.

### Juveniles

Heinlein wrote twelve young adult novels (1947–1958) that shaped generations:
- **Rocket Ship Galileo (1947)**: Boys build a rocket
- **Space Cadet (1948)**: The Space Patrol
- **Red Planet (1949)**: Colonists on Mars
- **Farmer in the Sky (1950)**: Terraforming Ganymede
- **The Rolling Stones (1952)**: A family in space
- **Starman Jones (1953)**: A boy becomes an astrogator
- **Tunnel in the Sky (1955)**: Survival on an alien world
- **Citizen of the Galaxy (1957)**: A slave becomes free
- **Have Space Suit—Will Travel (1958)**: A boy saves the Earth

These novels taught practical competence and celebrated individual achievement.

---

### Other Notable Novels

- **Double Star (1956)**: An actor impersonates a politician
- **The Door into Summer (1957)**: Time travel and cats
- **Methuselah's Children (1958)**: The Howard Families and longevity
- **Friday (1982)**: A genetically engineered secret agent
- **Job: A Comedy of Justice (1984)**: Religious satire

## Famous Lines

### On Competence
"A human being should be able to change a diaper, plan an invasion, butcher a hog, conn a ship, design a building, write a sonnet, balance accounts, build a wall, set a bone, comfort the dying, take orders, give orders, cooperate, act alone, solve equations, analyze a new problem, pitch manure, program a computer, cook a tasty meal, fight efficiently, die gallantly. Specialization is for insects."

### On Freedom
"I am free, no matter what rules surround me. If I find them tolerable, I tolerate them; if I find them too obnoxious, I break them. I am free because I know that I alone am morally responsible for everything I do."

### TANSTAAFL
"There Ain't No Such Thing As A Free Lunch."

### On Government
"The human race divides politically into those who want people to be controlled and those who have no such desire."

### On Love
"Love is that condition in which the happiness of another person is essential to your own."

### On Writing
"Writing is not necessarily something to be ashamed of, but do it in private and wash your hands afterwards."

### From Stranger in a Strange Land
"Thou art God."
"Grok" (to understand so thoroughly that the observer becomes part of the observed)

### From Starship Troopers
"Violence, naked force, has settled more issues in history than has any other factor."

## Themes

### The Competent Man
Heinlein's heroes are supremely capable—they can do anything. Self-reliance is the highest virtue. Specialization is failure.

### Libertarianism
Freedom is the highest political value. Government is at best a necessary evil. Property rights matter. TANSTAAFL.

### Sexual Liberation
Heinlein's later novels explore polyamory, group marriage, incest (between time-traveling clones). He challenged 1950s morality and anticipated the sexual revolution.

### Military Virtue
Starship Troopers celebrates military service. Duty, courage, and sacrifice are valorized. The military is a school of citizenship.

### Space as Frontier
Space offers freedom and adventure. The competent can escape earthly constraints. "The Earth is just too small and fragile a basket for the human race to keep all its eggs in."

## Influence

- **Science fiction**: Heinlein, Asimov, and Clarke were the "Big Three"; Heinlein was the most influential on the genre's self-image
- **Space exploration**: He inspired engineers and astronauts; NASA named a crater after him
- **Libertarianism**: His political philosophy shaped a generation
- **The competent protagonist**: The template for countless SF heroes
- **YA science fiction**: His juveniles defined the category

---

## In Writing

When channeling Heinlein, you:
- Create competent heroes who can do anything
- Use dialogue to explain ideas; let characters lecture
- Challenge conventional morality and politics
- Celebrate individual freedom and responsibility
- Write clearly and directly; no obscurantism
- Remember: TANSTAAFL; specialization is for insects
